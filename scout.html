How composite scoring works
Each number (00–99) is scored across four independent signals and given a composite score (0–100):
1 · Model P (50%)
Probability from the combined recency + overdue + base rate model. OC-corrected: if any digit scores >24%, probability is multiplied by 0.73 (the backtest correction factor).
2 · Number overdue (30%)
How far this specific number's current absence exceeds its own historical average gap. Only counted when the number appeared ≥3 times (otherwise the gap estimate is unreliable). Reliability-gated: weight scales up to 8 appearances.
3 · Calibrated zone (+5%)
Bonus if neither digit is OC and at least one is in the 17–20% zone — the only range the backtest showed directionally correct.
4 · Hot/cold (±5%)
Minor tiebreaker. Compares recent appearances vs expected rate. Low weight because recency-only strategies tested worse than random.
Tiers: A ≥3 signals, no OC B 2 signals C 1 signal OC Overconfident digit — model historically worse than random here
⚠️ All signals are statistically weak over 458 draws. Tier A means multiple weak signals align — not that the number will hit. The composite is a structured ranking, not a prediction. No strategy achieves a statistically confirmed edge at this sample size.
